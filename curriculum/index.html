Curriculum

Play, games, and the principles that underlie them have an essential role in building critical skills like systems thinking, creative problem solving, collaboration, empathy, and innovation. In this course, prepare to put your thinking caps on (both literal and metaphorical) and envision multiplayer games controlled by the human brain! Through weekly design fiction and game design exercises, students will become acquainted with emerging issues at the intersection of neuroscience, ethics, and society-and develop submissions to the Brains and Games International Design Fiction Competition on March 20th, 2021. The only requirement for this course is, naturally, an open mind.


Module 1: Designing our Minds (Part 1)

This module introduces the course goals, the Brains and Games International Design Fiction Competition, and the history of neurotechnology devices in academic research, clinical practice, industry, and the arts.


Module 2: Designing Desirable Futures

This module introduces Design Fiction as a method for exploring and critically analyzing possible futures through designed artifacts. Students start generating ideas for their Brains and Games submissions.


Module 3: Designing Delightful Experiences

This module introduces the basics of Game Design and, in general, User Experience Design. Students form teams and begin deliberating over the details of their Brains and Games submissions.


Module 4: Designing Great Pitches

This module introduces best practices for multimedia presentations, pitch design, and prototype development. Student teams draft presentations for their submission content, present these to each other, and reflect on needed preparation before submission.


Module 5: Designing Ethical Experiences

This module introduces Neuroethics and elaborates on emerging ethical, legal, and social issues in research, clinical, and commercial neurotechnology. Teams update their submissions/pitches to reflect lecture material and present their submission content to each other for critique.


Module 6: Designing Valuable Experiences

This module introduces the Neurotechnology Industry and the myriad disciplinary backgrounds supporting it. Students polish their presentation material before attending the Brains and Games public event.


Module 7: Designing Our Minds (Part 2)

This module encourages students to reflect on their class experience and frame their presention materials with a personal narrative about their experience in the class. Students send their final drafts to the instructor for class documentation.
